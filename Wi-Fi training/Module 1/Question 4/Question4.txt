A Wi-Fi Access Point (AP)  allows wireless devices to connect to a wired network using Wi-Fi.The functions of Wifi AP are:
-> Provides a wireless signal for devices to connect to the network.
-> Acts as a bridge between a wired Ethernet network and wireless devices.
-> Sends and receives radio signals between connected devices and the network.
-> Extends the coverage area of a Wi-Fi network, reducing dead zones.
-> Supports multiple simultaneous connections from various wireless devices.
-> Controls and optimizes bandwidth usage among connected devices.
-> Supports roaming
